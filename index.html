Hi, I’m Ananya! I work as an undergraduate research assistant at the Computational HCI lab advised by Dr. Nikola Banovic. I lead and contribute to projects supporting end-users through user literacy, critical reflection, and tech policy, which has equipped me with strong research skills and helped hone my interests. My research interests lie at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), specifically Human-AI Interaction (HAI), and Technology Policy. I aim to enhance support and reduce algorithmic harm for vulnerable communities, like older adults. Through socio-cognitive theories, design, and policy frameworks, I explore how to create safe HAI. I am passionate about augmenting human-centered design to address multi-stakeholder issues, particularly at the intersection of AI and vulnerability.

I expect to graduate with a BS in Computer Science from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, in 2025. I am applying to PhD programs this Fall and am excited to work on research at the intersection of HCI, Tech Policy, and community impact! I’d love to connect to discuss overlapping interests.
